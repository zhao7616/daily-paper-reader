Abstract Background: General aviation fleet expansion demands intelligent health monitoring under computational constraints. Real-world aircraft health diagnosis requires balancing accuracy with computational constraints under extreme class imbalance and environmental uncertainty. Existing end-to-end approaches suffer from the receptive field paradox: global attention intro-duces excessive operational heterogeneity noise for fine-grained fault classification, while localized constraints sacrifice critical cross-temporal context essential for anomaly detection. 

Methods: This paper presents an AI-driven heterogeneous cascading architecture for general aviation health management. The proposed Long-Micro Scale Diagnostician (LMSD) explicitly decouples global anomaly detection (full-sequence attention) from micro-scale fault classifica-tion (restricted receptive fields), resolving the receptive field paradox while minimizing training overhead. A knowledge distillation-based interpretability module provides physically traceable explanations for safety-critical validation. 

Results: Experiments on the public National General Aviation Flight Information Database (NGAFID) dataset (28,935 flights, 36 categories) demonstrate 4–8% improvement in safety-critical metrics (MCWPM) with 4.2 × training acceleration and 46% model compression com-pared to end-to-end baselines. 

Conclusions: The AI-driven heterogeneous architecture offers deployable solutions for avi-ation equipment health management, with potential for digital twin integration in future work. The proposed framework substantiates deployability in resource-constrained aviation environ-ments while maintaining stringent safety requirements.

1. Introduction 

Artificial intelligence (AI) is reshaping modern aviation equipment maintenance paradigms. This paper presents an AI-driven heterogeneous cascading architecture for general aviation health management, addressing the critical challenge of balancing diagnostic accuracy with computational constraints under extreme class imbalance and environmental uncertainty. Gen-eral aviation fleet expansion and aging aircraft retention have intensified the tension between flight safety assurance and maintenance cost control under stringent onboard computational budgets 1–5 . Whole-aircraft health diagnosis—discerning operational anomalies and identify-ing specific fault modes in real time—demands ultra-low latency decisions under extreme class imbalance and limited hardware resources. Existing methods predominantly rely on ideal-ized simulations (e.g., C-MAPSS) assuming pristine data and unambiguous labels 6–10 , whereas real-world operational datasets such as National General Aviation Flight Information Database (NGAFID) exhibit substantial uncertainty: noisy measurements from legacy avionics, ambiguous maintenance-derived labels, and multi-source environmental interference pervade flight logs 11,12 .This idealization-reality gap compromises the reliability of conventional end-to-end approaches when deployed in resource-constrained aviation environments 13–15 .The NGAFID dataset—comprising 23-dimensional flight profiles from an operational Cessna 172 fleet—exemplifies three inherent challenges of real-world aviation PHM: uncertainty (envi-ronmental noise and label ambiguity), target component sparsity (fault signatures submerged in low-variance subspaces), and multi-stage coupling (dynamic interdependencies masking local faults) 9,16 . These characteristics necessitate divergent architectural responses: discriminating operational heterogeneity requires full-sequence receptive fields to capture cross-temporal con-texts, whereas isolating fault-specific patterns demands restricted fields to suppress cross-phase noise 1,17 . Aviation safety regulations prioritize missed detection over false alarms, imposing ultra-low false negative requirements. However, severe class imbalance—where healthy flights dominate and faults follow long-tailed distributions—renders end-to-end training on complete datasets computationally prohibitive while naive augmentation risks distribution bias 17,18 . The imperative to minimize training overhead while maintaining safety-critical performance under hardware constraints renders collaborative optimization of heterogeneous subtasks an unresolved engineering challenge. Data-driven fault diagnosis has achieved substantial progress across subsystem-level applica-tions including rotating machinery, turbine engines, and energy storage systems 11,19–31 , evolving from traditional signal processing techniques to modern deep architectures 32–46 . Notably, Li et al. 47 demonstrated significant RUL prediction improvements through spatial-temporal attention mechanisms in turbofan engines under controlled conditions. However, such end-to-end architec-tures inherently conflate global operational pattern discrimination with local fault characteriza-tion, suffering from the receptive field paradox: global attention introduces excessive operational heterogeneity noise for fine-grained fault classification, while localized constraints sacrifice critical cross-temporal context essential for anomaly detection 17,48 . Recent comprehensive reviews on in-telligent fault diagnosis methods toward gas turbines have identified interpretability and rigorous 2validation as critical gaps for aviation applications, documenting the progression from conven-tional signal processing to deep learning architectures in industrial PHM systems 49 . Yet these advances predominantly operate under idealized component-level, closed-set assumptions 50,51 ,lacking explicit architectural mechanisms to reconcile training economy with deployment feasi-bility under whole-aircraft level uncertainty and computational constraints. 

Architectural conflicts in task adaptability. End-to-end approaches enforce implicit global-local trade-offs within monolithic parameter spaces, fundamentally compromising both objectives. Contemporary cross-scale interactive attention networks merely enforce implicit trade-offs rather than resolving the competing receptive field requirements of global operational context modeling versus local fault signature isolation 1,48 .

Interpretability and credibility constraints. Aviation certification demands trans-parency beyond performance metrics—requiring demonstrable physical evidence of decision mechanisms 2. While recent advances in knowledge-data hybrid methods demonstrate that em-bedding physical fingerprints can improve assessment robustness in aeroengine gas path moni-toring 52 , existing deep architectures remain predominantly black-box 53 , and post-hoc techniques (SHAP, LIME) struggle to integrate with physical fault propagation pathways 12,42,54 .

Computational efficiency and deployability bottlenecks. Real-world deployment im-poses strict constraints on model size, training overhead, and inference latency. Training complex end-to-end models on massive imbalanced datasets incurs prohibitive computational costs, while the absence of data scale decoupling mechanisms prevents feasible fleet-level retraining 4,5 . Ex-isting methods lack explicit architectural pathways to reconcile “large-sample lightweight” global screening with “small-sample complex” local diagnosis. This paper addresses these engineering constraints through the Diagnosis Decomposition Framework (DDF), instantiated as the Long-Micro Scale Diagnostician (LMSD): 1. Heterogeneous Task Decomposition : Explicit architectural decoupling of anomaly detection (AD) and fault classification (FC) resolves the receptive field paradox. Full-sequence Convolutional Tokenizer with Multi-Head Self-Attention (ConvTokMHSA) han-dles operational context discrimination, while restricted-receptive-field Multi-Micro Kernel Network (MMK Net) extracts local fault features—enabling optimal resource allocation between data scales. 2. Interpretability-by-Design for Safety-Critical Deployment : The Keyness Extrac-tion Layer (KEL) leverages knowledge distillation to furnish physically traceable, two-stage independent explanations, disentangling operational pattern attention from fault-specific localization to address aviation credibility requirements. 3. Efficient Decoupled Training Strategy : Hard-isolated training with frozen-parameter cascading pairs large-sample lightweight models (AD) with small-sample complex models (FC), minimizing missed detections while substantially reducing training time and storage overhead under resource constraints. 2. Data Description 

This study employs the NGAFID aviation maintenance dataset as the analytical subject 9.Comprising 23-dimensional sensor time-series (1 Hz sampling) from over 28,000 flights (31,000 hours) of a Cessna 172 fleet across 36 unscheduled maintenance categories, NGAFID captures real-world operational uncertainty absent in simulation environments. Its sensor network cov-ers whole-aircraft subsystems with multi-system coupling characteristics, constituting a whole-airframe level diagnosis dataset. The 23-dimensional sensors partition into two complementary observation sets (Table 1): monitoring data (electrical, fuel, engine, cylinder status) directly reflects powerplant and avionics health; operational pattern data (IAS/VSpd/AltMSL/OAT) characterizes flight profiles, piloting styles, and meteorological conditions. This binary partition reveals the core characteristic of aviation flight parameter data—monitoring data normal envelopes and anomalous deviations are nested within specific operational patterns, necessitating context-dependent discrimination rather than absolute thresholds. NGAFID comprises the benchmark Subset (19 classes, 11,446 flights) and Overall dataset (36 classes, 28,935 flights), both exhibiting dual imbalance: healthy/anomalous quantitative disparity and extreme long-tailed fault distributions (head classes >2,000 flights versus <15 for tails). This reflects objective fleet reality—chronic wear predominates over acute failures— rather than sampling bias. 3. Methodology 

This section presents the Diagnosis Decomposition Framework (DDF) and its instantiation, the Long-Micro Scale Diagnostician (LMSD). We formalize the theoretical foundations of task decomposition (Section 3.1), design the meta-architecture with heterogeneous cascading (Section 3.2), detail the component implementations (Section 3.3), and establish the Keyness Extraction Layer for interpretability (Section 3.4).

These subtasks exhibit competing Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) maximization objectives: global fields optimize AD via cross-temporal context integration, while local fields optimize FC via fault-signal isolation. The receptive field paradox renders single architectures fundamen-tally incapable of simultaneous optimization—global attention introduces excessive operational heterogeneity noise for FC, while local constraints sacrifice critical context for AD. 3.2. LMSD: Architectural Instantiation of DDF 

The Long-Micro Scale Diagnostician (LMSD) instantiates DDF through explicit heteroge-neous cascading. Long denotes the global stage ( fAD ) with receptive fields ≈ L for operational pattern detection; Micro denotes the local stage ( fF C ) with receptive fields ≪ L for fault-related micro-period extraction. LMSD implements the meta-architecture operator M via Hard-Threshold Routing with Dimensional Isolation. For input x = [ m ∥ o]:

Stage 1 (Long/Global Screening). The Health Analyzer HθH performs AD, outputting logits zAD = [ zh, z a]⊤. Probability distribution pAD = Softmax( zAD ) = [ ph, p a]⊤ determines the routing path. 

Stage 2 (Routing and Dimensional Isolation). LMSD constructs an (N +1) -dimensional output vector zD (dimension 0 for healthy, 1 : N for faults). The routing mechanism enforces hard isolation: 

• Healthy Path (zh > z a): Dimension 0 set to zh, remaining dimensions masked to −∞ ,ensuring zero fault probability mass. 

• Anomalous Path (za ≥ zh): Dimension 0 masked to −∞ , with dimensions 1 : N populated by the Fault Analyzer output zF C .This guarantees healthy samples never enter the fault classifier’s discrimination space, while fault distributions remain uninfluenced by the healthy category. 

Stage 3 (Micro/Local Precise Diagnosis). The Fault Analyzer FθF processes confirmed anomalies, outputting zF C = [ z1, . . . , z N ]⊤. The final vector zD = [ −∞ , z⊤ 

> F C

]⊤ undergoes Soft-max normalization to yield pD.

Decoupled Training Strategy. Diverging from end-to-end joint optimization, LMSD employs data-level decomposition : HθH trains on the complete dataset D (leveraging all healthy samples for global pattern learning), while FθF trains exclusively on the anomalous subset 

Da = {(xi, y F C i ) | yAD i = 1 } (eliminating healthy sample contamination of fault discrimination 8space). Optimization-level hard isolation prohibits gradient backpropagation across the routing boundary, preventing fine-grained FC gradients from interfering with global AD pattern learning. During inference, pre-trained HθH and FθF execute with frozen parameters. This “large-sample lightweight” (AD) versus “small-sample complex” (FC) strategy reduces training iteration overhead while aligning model complexity with data scale. Heterogeneous hy-perparameter configurations (hidden dimensions, encoder depths) accommodate divergent de-mands of global context modeling versus local feature extraction.

3.4. Keyness Extraction Layer via Distillation Learning

Unlike post-hoc techniques (SHAP, LIME) that struggle to integrate with temporal physical mechanisms, KEL extracts sample-level temporal keyness through knowl-edge distillation, enabling verification of whether model attention aligns with fault propagation pathways. 4. Experimental Results and Discussion 

4.1. Data Processing and Experimental Setup 

Experiments are conducted on the NGAFID Overall dataset (36 classes, 28,935 flights), with the benchmark Subset (19 classes) results provided in Supplementary Material. All variable-length flight segments are normalized to a fixed length of 2048 via Cubic Spline Interpolation, retaining complete 23-dimensional records with <10% missing channel rates. Missing values are addressed via Forward Fill. Z-score normalization parameters are computed exclusively from training sets to prevent statistical leakage. 

Validation Protocol: Stratified 5-Fold Cross-Validation is employed with physical file iso-lation between folds. For end-to-end models, four folds are used for training (9:1 internal split), with the remaining fold held out for evaluation. For LMSD, the Health Analyzer and Fault An-alyzer share the complete training data per fold but execute independent 9:1 training/validation splits internally, ensuring optimization isolation while maintaining test set consistency. 

Training Protocols: Conventional end-to-end methods employ early stopping (patience=3). LMSD adopts hard-isolated decoupled training: HθH trains on the complete dataset (leverag-ing all healthy samples), while FθF trains exclusively on the anomalous subset Da, preventing healthy sample contamination of fault discrimination space. Heterogeneous hyperparameter configurations for all models are detailed in Supplementary Material .

Class Imbalance Mitigation: Proportional replication augmentation ( kda = 3 ) is applied to FC and Diagnosis tasks: Ncda = min(3 × Nc, N cmax ), preventing distribution distortion while 11 ensuring tail-class learnability. 

4.2. Evaluation Metrics 

A four-dimensional evaluation system assesses: (1) classification performance; (2) training efficiency; (3) inference overhead; (4) safety-critical metrics. 

Classification Metrics: Accuracy (ACC), macro-averaged F1-score (F1), and Weighted F1 (WF1) for end-to-end tasks. 

Efficiency Metrics: Epoch Training Time (ET), Total Training Time (TTT), Inference Time for 32 Samples (IT32, post GPU warm-up), and Model Size (MSize)—quantifying fleet-level retraining feasibility and edge-node deployability. 

Safety-Critical Metrics: For AD, False Negative Rate (FNR). For end-to-end Diagnosis, Multi-Class Weighted Penalty Metric (MCWPM) formalizes “Safety-over-Economy” constraints:

4.3. Baseline Models 

Baselines span three technical routes: (1) Bi-LSTM : 3-layer bidirectional LSTM (hid-den=128) for sequence dependency modeling 34 ; (2) InceptionTime : Multi-scale convolutional kernels (10/20/40) for local feature extraction 56 ; (3) InceptionTimeAttn : CNN-Transformer hybrid with self-attention for global-local fusion. All models undergo systematic hyperparameter search (Supplementary Material) for fair comparison.

4.4.1. Task Adaptability: AD versus FC 

Table 3 validates the architectural hypothesis: global receptive fields for AD, local fields for FC . ConvTokMHSA achieves optimal AD performance (ACC 0.7657) via full-sequence attention, but collapses in FC (ACC 0.3791) due to cross-stage noise. Conversely, MMK Net’s restricted receptive fields excel at FC (F1 0.5202) but underperform in AD. This diametric opposition substantiates the receptive field paradox: no single architecture reconciles global discrimination with local extraction .

4.4.2. Integrated Diagnosis: Accuracy, Safety, and Efficiency 

Table 4 compares end-to-end baselines against LMSD on the integrated Diagnosis task (36 classes). LMSD achieves the highest safety-critical MCWPM (0.6148) and F1 (0.4091), outper-forming end-to-end baselines by 4–8% in MCWPM. While ConvTokSWLA achieves marginally better ACC (0.6424), its lower MCWPM (0.5712) indicates that implicit global-local trade-offs fail to mitigate missed detection risks as effectively as explicit architectural decoupling. This validates the “large-sample lightweight + small-sample complex” resource allocation strategy, enhancing deployment feasibility under fleet-level computational constraints.

This disparity between “confusability” and “outlierness” reflects three fundamental dataset constraints: 

Label noise from maintenance records. NGAFID’s retrospective labeling (anomalous = pre-maintenance, healthy = post-maintenance) induces False-Negatives (latent defects in “healthy” flights) and False-Positives (preventive vs. actual faults). This aleatoric uncertainty, compounded by epistemic uncertainty from scarce samples, constrains prognostic reliability 57 .DDF heterogeneously attenuates this: AD trains on full data for robustness, while FC—restricted to the anomalous subset—suffers amplified noise, limiting tail-class learning. 

Small samples and extreme long-tail distribution. With only 28,935 flights across 36 categories spanning three orders of magnitude (head >4,000 vs. tail <20), proportional replication ( kda = 3 ) merely interpolates within the original manifold without expanding tail-class learnability. MMK Net achieves <40% recall for categories with <30 samples, indicating fundamental data volume constraints. 

Limited sensor capacity and feature sparsity. The Cessna-172’s 23-dimensional 1Hz ac-quisition pales against modern QAR systems, submerging fault features in process noise. PCA confirms discriminative signals concentrate in the 12th–18th minor components (low-variance subspaces). While LMSD’s receptive field separation mitigates cross-stage noise, it cannot over-come the physical information bottleneck imposed by sensor sparsity. These tri-coupled constraints indicate that LMSD’s metrics approach the discriminability boundary under current data limitations. Future breakthroughs require: (1) refined maintenance 18 semantic annotation; (2) enhanced flight parameter quality/capacity; (3) component-subsystem-whole-aircraft multi-level architectures with physics-informed constraints. 

5. Conclusion and Future Work 

To address data uncertainty, task heterogeneity, and computational constraints in general aviation health diagnosis, this paper proposes the Diagnosis Decomposition Framework (DDF) instantiated as the Long-Micro Scale Diagnostician (LMSD). The core contribution is a deploy-able methodology resolving the receptive field paradox through explicit architectural decoupling: lightweight global analyzers with full-sequence fields (ConvTokMHSA) learn operational patterns from large-scale data, while complex micro-scale analyzers with restricted fields (MMK Net) extract local fault features from anomalous samples. This “large-sample lightweight + small-sample complex” strategy reduces training overhead while ensuring stringent missed detection constraints via hard-threshold routing, formalizing aviation’s “safety-first” ethics into deployable AI design. NGAFID validation confirms effectiveness across three dimensions: (1) Task adaptabil-ity —heterogeneous cascading achieves 4–8% MCWPM improvement over end-to-end baselines under class imbalance; (2) Interpretability —KEL visualizations confirm physically traceable hierarchical cognition (global operational discrimination enabling microscopic fault localization); (3) Computational efficiency —decoupled training reduces TTT by 4.2 × versus complex end-to-end alternatives with 46% smaller model size. However, label noise, extreme long-tail distri-butions, and limited sensor capacity impose performance ceilings, indicating that methodological advances must coordinate with data infrastructure improvements. Future work advances along three axes: (1) Methodological —evolving from hard-threshold to uncertainty-quantified soft routing with physics-informed embeddings for causal reasoning; (2) Data infrastructure —enhanced flight parameter capacity via increased sensor dimen-sions/sampling rates and refined maintenance annotations; (3) Engineering deployment —edge computing adaptation, online incremental learning, and cross-domain transfer capabilities for fleet-wide autonomous health management. 

Digital Twin Integration. Future extensions of this work will explore the integration with digital twin frameworks, where the proposed heterogeneous diagnosis architecture can serve as the real-time analytics engine for virtual aircraft replicas, enabling closed-loop health manage-ment. In such a framework, the LMSD’s two-stage decision process would feed into continuously updated digital twins, providing both instantaneous fault alerts and long-term degradation pre-dictions. The physically traceable interpretability provided by KEL aligns naturally with digital twin requirements for transparent, auditable decision pathways, while the computational effi-ciency of decoupled training enables real-time synchronization between physical assets and their virtual counterparts.
